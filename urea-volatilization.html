Shaded rows (day 8+) are lower-confidence — forecast skill, especially for rain, drops after about a week. Humidity stands in for surface wetness (it's forecast across the full window, unlike soil moisture).

How it works. A daily loss accrues from a susceptible pool of applied urea-N until a ≥0.5 in rain incorporates it into the soil and shuts loss off. The daily rate scales with soil temperature (urease activity ~doubles per 10°C), relative humidity as a surface-wetness proxy, soil pH, texture/CEC, and tillage. NBPT suppresses loss during a temperature-dependent lag (~7–14 days) — buying time for rain. If no rain arrives within the lag, losses resume, which is why a 5-day-only view over-sells NBPT and the pay-off verdict is judged over the full 14-day episode.
